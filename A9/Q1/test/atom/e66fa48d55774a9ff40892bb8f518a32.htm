at 3:46 PM
I gave in to a pumpkin spice latte. I figure, it's November, it's time, this once, to indulge the senses in fake nutmeg and cinnamon.

I'm doing that thing where I have my headphones in my ears but I'm not listening to anything.

I came out to escape the mess in my apartment and to hand-write a letter, and I'll get to it, but I need to do this, too, first. I need to write without purpose, pressure, pain.

Last night I was buzzed with caffeine and I just burned through this extra hour without a second thought, watching Catching Fire until the wee hours. Then I woke and looked at my clock to see it was 5, which meant it was really 4. WELP.

I was walking down the boulevard yesterday thinking that it would be nice to have a place to dance that was not a crowded club or a dingy dive bar with a 9x9 dance floor caked in beer, or even a wedding reception. Does such a place existence? Maybe this place is secretly my kitchen.
Sometimes you just have to dance it out.

At three AM I went through the blogs I follow and removed most of the inactive ones,,, though keeping the ones of friends and family, even if they, you, haven't updated in years. Because I am nostalgic like that.

The latte is gone, and so are my thoughts, for now.

It's the long, sustained aftershock that rocks you back to sleep.
