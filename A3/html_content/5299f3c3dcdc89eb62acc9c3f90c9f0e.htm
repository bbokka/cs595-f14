Easy Homemade Tomato Basil Parmesan Soup

October 3, 2014 By Meal Plan Mom (Brenda) Leave a Comment

Ever since I discovered how easy it is to make homemade tomato soup, it has become a favorite around our house. We especially like to have this soup on Sunday evenings after a busy weekend. It’s so simple and quick to whip up and then we pair it with a grilled cheese sandwich–super yum!

I tweaked a recipe inspired by Jenny Hobick’s Everyday Occasionas blog to create this Easy Homemade Tomato Basil Parmesan Soup and once you try it, you may never go back to the premade canned stuff! We actually love the soup from LaMadeleine also but at nearly $13.00 for three 15 oz. jars, making your own can be a lot cheaper too. Plus, you can choose to use organic canned tomatoes or even tomatoes from your garden you’ve canned yourself. Give it a try and let me know what you think!

Print
Easy Homemade Tomato Basil Parmesan Soup

Ingredients

  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 - 14 oz. cans crushed tomatoes, undrained
  • 1 - 14 oz. can petite diced tomatoes, undrained
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp fresh ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup milk (2% will give you a creamier soup but even a skim is ok!)
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/4 cup grated parmesan cheese

Instructions

  1. In large saucepan, saute garlic in olive oil on medium heat one about minute, until golden but being careful not to burn the garlic. Add the crushed tomatoes and diced tomatoes. Add broth, salt, pepper and sugar. Bring soup to a boil then simmer on medium for 10 minutes.
  2. Turn heat to low and stir in milk, basil and parmesan. Top with shredded parmesan if desired.
