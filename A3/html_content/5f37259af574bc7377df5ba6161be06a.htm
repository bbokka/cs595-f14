End of a Chapter, Beginning of Another.

It's been a fun 1.5 years of college, and I must say I've enjoyed it thoroughly. Sure, it wasn't easy, since Concord took me outside my comfort zone and I had to adapt and find my niche within it. Of course, I wasn't alone. I had my friends, family and teachers to help me out, people who I could turn to when I needed them. They helped me grow as a person by teaching me new things, and indeed I tried many things for the first time when I was in Concord such as rock climbing, frisbee, guitar, cooking, Dota and a whole lot of other activities, all because of the people around me.

But, all good things must come to an end. Concord was a short and sweet chapter (in relation to Lodge etc.), and has come to a close. The next chapter will be much longer, possibly better, possibly worse, but definitely different. I'll meet new people, learn new things, have new experiences and who knows what else! I'm really interested to know what the future holds, but at the same time, full of doubts and uncertainties, but I suppose that's part and parcel of starting a new chapter in life.

I look forward to life in Cambridge, even though I know it'll be filled with stress, hard work and sleepless nights, because in the end, I'm sure it'll all be worth it.

Grad Trip

Ok this post is probably a little(very) late, but as the saying goes: better late than never. The trip basically consisted of 2 main parts: Penang and Rawa(an island off the coast of Johor). I arrived in Penang on the morning of August 1st, and departed from Johor on the morning of August 9th. I must say, this trip was completely worth the time it took to convince my parents to allow me to go on. Good food, good company, good experiences. For slightly more than a week, I was allowed to live life without any worries about university, results or A-levels but sadly, all good things must come to an end. I sincerely hope that my friendship with this group of people is the exception to that rule, because I really appreciate them and the things they've done for me.

Monday, August 18, 2014

Kindness

When was the last time you made a person's day? Our lives are so busy and we are so used to putting ourselves first that often, we fail to see that others are in need of help. My life has become increasingly boring, with nothing to do every day, so I decided to take an interest in the lives of others. (That just made me sound like a stalker) I decided to give my time to others and help them solve their problems and failing that, to just lend a listening ear and speak a few words of comfort. Believe me, I haven't felt this good in ages, especially when people thank me for my help or say that they feel better. Like I said before in a previous post, I'm happy when others are happy. Some may criticise my way of thinking, calling it naive and they might tell me that the real world is cruel, but that is exactly why we should all help each other out. I recall an Enid Blyton story I read when I was younger, about a kindness chain where people passed on kindness they received to someone else; kindness eventually made it back to the person who started the chain. Give it a try, spend some time doing something selfless and mark my words, you'll enjoy it and bring a smile to someone else as well as yourself.

Friday, August 15, 2014

Results

Well, results day has come and gone. I expected the results to arrive at 3pm, but instead, Concord decided to send them an hour earlier, so I was extremely surprised when I checked my phone after my Dota game finished and saw everyone talking about results. A quick check of my inbox confirmed that results were indeed out, and so, my heart racing, I opened the email and downloaded the PDF files of my results.

The moment I saw my A* for physics, I was instantly relieved. The biggest part of my offer: the A*, had been met. I didn't really care any more what I got for everything else, my AS UMS were definitely sufficient to confirm that I would get the A's that I needed to meet my A*AA offer. However, I could not help but feel slightly disappointed with my biology result, as I missed out on the A* because of my EMPA. I thought the paper was pretty standard, but my marks said otherwise: I received a C, 38/60. This meant my A2 modules for biology totalled 266/300, I missed the A* by 4 UMS. In the heat of the moment, I seriously considered sending it for a re-mark, but thankfully, my brain was not completely dead and I reminded myself that doing so would serve no purpose, as I had already met my offer.

I have often asked myself the question: "Am I happy?" and on 14th August 2014, I could finally answer confidently: "Yes I am".

Saturday, August 9, 2014

I Cried Yesterday

I cried yesterday. For the first time in what seems like an eternity, my thoughts were not organised and sharp. Instead, they mixed freely together until I could no longer think clearly but instead, saw flashes here and there of different memories.There was nothing I could do. I was helpless, vulnerable and pathetic.

I cried because I realised that the future will never be like the past. I cried because I realised that some of the most important people to me now, will no longer be a part of my life a few years down the road and the thought of that honestly scares me.

There is a saying that humans are not afraid of the dark, we are afraid of the unknown. Perhaps that is why I cling to the past and dread the future. If my life were a journey, Lodge would be a tiny village; warm and cozy. Moving to Concord would be like taking my first steps outside the boundaries and walking to a town, albeit a small one, with a faster pace of life and exciting sparks of adventure. What lies ahead? I don't know. I honestly don't know what to expect. The town and the village were safe and comfortable, but now I have to leave them, and venture out to the unknown. I don't want to leave. I want to stay here forever, but I know that I have to move on and the only way to do that is to leave. That, ladies and gentlemen, is why I cried yesterday.

Saturday, July 26, 2014

Ignored

Sometimes, I wish there was another one of me. Just so that there would be someone who I could talk to when I most need it, who listens and understands, who shares my views and knows how I feel. It's just so discouraging when you take the initiative to message someone first and you're completely ignored. Guess it's something I need to get used to. Before anyone accuses me of being demanding, I'd like to point out that you should have the courtesy to at least tell someone straight to their face if they're bothering you, simply because it's better for both parties that way. You stop getting bothered and the other person gets feedback that you don't like talking to them. 

I've had enough of this. I've said it before, I prefer it when people are direct. And even when they aren't, they're easy to read. Perhaps it's time I find out who actually enjoys and misses my company.

Thursday, July 17, 2014

People and Opinions

I can't help it. This urge to correct people when they are so obviously wrong. To point out that what they're saying makes no sense. This wouldn't matter if everyone were like me and remained open-minded and were willing to change their opinions when proven wrong, but the fact remains that people like their opinions, so much that they cling on to them and stubbornly refuse to budge.

I once had this argument with someone where I asked them yes/no questions and with their answers, arrived at a perfectly logical conclusion which opposed their opinion. Guess what? They chose to stick with their opinion instead of admitting they were wrong. Is pride really that important? Is it so difficult for us to say that we are wrong? I have had my mind changed on many things before, in case you were thinking I'm a hypocrite. If people show me evidence and use logic to show me that my opinions are flawed, I will thank them graciously and revise my opinion.

Some call this a flaw, I prefer to think of it as my natural instinct to eliminate ignorance. People may hate me for this, but so be it. After all, if you aren't hated by anyone, you're doing something wrong.
