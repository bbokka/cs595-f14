Video

It’s not just about drinking and driving, though. 35,000 people per year die in car crashes in the U.S., and a large number of those are people just driving beyond their means to control their vehicle, or not paying attention. I see it all the time in real life, so many *very near misses* (and a few actual crashes) because someone is just simply not thinking about anything beyond the end of their nose. Not drinking and driving is really important, but there is a lot to driving responsibly that goes way beyond simply being sober, and we tend to completely ignore that, implying that the only way to be irresponsible while driving is to be drunk. So many peoples’ lives are destroyed in a split second just because a person driving couldn’t wait 3 seconds to pass, or had to drive that extra 10mph, or was putting on lipstick, or READING THE NEWSPAPER or just turned without checking first. It’s enough people dying that it’s like U.S. involvement in a moderately sized global war every year. So please, really take it seriously when you get behind the wheel of a car, it’s serious business.
