Apple announces iPhone 6 and iPhone 6 Plus

Leave a reply

Originally posted on Metro:

Apple CEO Tim Cook announces the iPhone 6 during an Apple special event (Picture: Getty Images)

Apple has revealed its new smartphones: the iPhone 6 and iPhone 6 Plus.

The gadgets were announced at a special event in Cupertino, California, earlier today by the company’s CEO Tim Cook.

The devices were revealed at the same location where Steve Jobs gave us a first viewing of the Mac three decades ago.

Guests prepare for the start of an Apple event (Picture: AP)

The iPhone 6 will have a 4.7-inch screen, while the iPhone 6 Plus will be 5.5 inches.

The screen resolution on the Plus version will be sharper than previous iPhones, at 401 pixels per inch rather than 326.

Apple is taking advantage of the larger screen by offering a horizontal view of the home screen. Usually, icons are stacked vertically.

Apple was also expected to unveil wearable technology.

Originally posted on Metro:

Mesut Ozil sustained an ankle injury during Arsenal’s game against Leicester City (Picture: REUTERS)

Mesut Ozil is a doubt to feature in Arsenal’s Premier League clash against Manchester City in a week’s time after struggling to recover from an ankle injury.

The 25-year-old suffered the injury towards the end of The Gunners’ 1-1 draw away to Leicester City at the weekend, which subsequently ruled him out of Germany’s 4-2 loss to Argentina on Wednesday.

According to The Daily Mail, the midfielder flew out late to meet up with his international teammates with Germany manager, Joachim Low, later confirming that Ozil is ‘doubtful’ to play in the team’s Euro 2016 qualifier against Scotland on Sunday.

The extent of Ozil’s injury has not yet been confirmed, but it is believed that he may not be fully fit in time for Arsenal’s league game against City at the Emirates on 13 September.

Thibaut Courtois reveals he will pen new Chelsea deal after international break

Leave a reply

Originally posted on Metro:

Thibaut Courtois will sign a new five-year deal with Chelsea (Picture: Getty)

Thibaut Courtois is set to sign a new five-year deal with Chelsea when he returns from international duty.

The Belgian goalkeeper has kept Blues legend Petr Cech out of the side since his return from a three-year loan spell with Atletico Madrid this summer.

And now Courtois’ position as Chelsea’s first-choice stopper is expected to be confirmed with a bumper new deal.

‘It’s not yet signed,’ Courtois told Sky Sports.

‘I am here with the national team so it is difficult, but when I am back at Chelsea I will probably sign the new contract.’

Courtois has helped Jose Mourinho’s side to maximum points from their opening three Premier League games this season, as the Blues top the table ahead of Swansea, who they face at Stamford Bridge next weekend.
