October Top Blogger #1 - Lucie - Fat Beauty X


Welcome to my first October Top Blogger.

The gorgeous Lucie from Fat Beauty X.

I first found Lucie's blog through twitter as someone retweeted her YouTube blog post, I think it was a Primark Haul and I was hooked. She is a natural in front of the camera and I love her style too.

I was lucky to meet Lucie in the flesh when I was in Ms British Beauty Curve in the Summer and she is as nice in person as she is on her YouTube. 

When I asked Lucie if she would be one of my Top Bloggers in October she was really happy to be a part of it. 

Question 1. What was the inspiration behind your blog?

I've always watched loads of youtube videos both for make up tips and inspiration, as embarrassing as it sounds, I used to search for other larger ladies talking about contouring and highlighting for rounder faces, or ways to hide my double chin. I suffer with POS so I have an untold amount of facial hair so I used to search a lot about high coverage makeup. I got asked all the time for tips and advice and eventually figured 'well why cant I be one of the people giving the advice?' I'd spent years searching others, Why not be willing to help others and be the help that I was once looking for?! So Fatbeautyx was born!

I had always been interested in fashion and found I had an abundance of confidence and would always cross the boundaries in terms of wearing things I as a fat girl "shouldn't wear". I got complemented a lot on my outfits so I eventually started posting about fashion as well as beauty.

Question 2. You vlog as well as blog, when did you make the leap in to making videos? 
Back in December 2013, I had finally decided to take the plunge and do videos along side my blog. At the risk of sounding remotely big headed haha I'm quite a character and very sarcastic and I'd like to think I type how I'd talk if we were face to face and I wanted to have that connection with my followers so that they could get more of a feel for me. I pride myself on being honest and 100% myself so doing videos helped me achieve that sense of intimacy with my followers that I didn't get in my posts. Plus I think its easy to read something wrong or take someones personality the wrong way, so doing videos for me allowed me to portray my banterful self so that it wouldn't be taken in the wrong way.

Question 3. You have such an amazing following on both your blog and YouTube. What would you say makes a successful blogger/vlogger?

haha I think it's different for everyone but in terms of videos? I think its because I'm a character. I'm forever being told in my videos that I'm funny and I film my videos like I'm a friend talking to them chilling on my bed telling them about my new lipstick or something. I think lighting, sound come into play too. I know as someone who watched videos for years if the lighting was bad or the sound quality wasn't that good I wouldn't really return back, and as I said for me, I love watching someone animated, someone I can relate to, so someone being themselves always wins me over. 

In terms of my blog, I think I'm in a minority, posting about both plus size fashion and beauty, I don't think too many cross over as much as myself, maybe the odd makeup bit here and there but I've got a 50/50 mix so I think that helps. I spend a lot of time on social media posting about a recent posts and I try and get involved. I comment on a lot of posts and take part in twitter chats when I can so having 4000+ followers across my social media hasn't just dropped on my lap, I have worked hard for each one :)

Question 4. What is your proudest moment with your blog/vlog so far? 

Oh god I can only pick one moment? I've been so lucky to have the opportunities I have had in this last year and as much as I'd love to say reaching X amount of followers, or getting to work with brands like Newlook, a brand I've been buying from for over 10 years! I think ultimately my proudest moment was after I had written a post about my POS and showing how bad my facial hair is and just talking about my feelings (you can catch up with her latest post on that here), ways I got rid of my hair and my makeup routine to hide it, and conceal the marks, lumps and bumps. I received an email from one of my followers with her telling me how much the post helped her, How she was being bullied with this and watching my video helped her so much. For me, getting to help someone, being the help I had looked for when I was doing my youtube searches... It's just a feeling beyond words. :D

Question 5. How would your friends describe you? 

I think most of my friends would describe me as bubbly, funny, quick witted, Kind hearted, reliable and FARRRR too open :P  



You can catch Lucies Vlog on YouTube here and if you don't already follow on Twitter @FatBeautyX or on Instagram @FatBeautyX

A good nights sleep


Some of you may not know this but I am an asthma sufferer.

My asthma is only a recent condition which I have had for about 3 years.

It started off with my allergy to cat dander but I still now need my inhalers without living with cats. I do have it under control but at night time I often have to have my inhaler by my bedside and I do use it during the night often.

Recently I was contact by Silk Bedding Direct to try out one of their duvets as I had mentioned I was in need of a good nights sleep.


Let me tell you a little bit about these duvets first:

The duvets are filled with 100% Mulberry Silk, they are not silk on the outside. They have a wonderful soft cotton sateen outercasing though which is hand stitched, not that horrible scratchy stuff most cheap duvets have on the outside.

When it arrived it came in a beautiful case, which can be used for storing it away when it is no longer required (it is a summer duvet so a winter one would be required in the colder months, however they do have an all weather one which I believe would be just fine).



The neat thing about these is they have these little catches on so if you wanted to double the duvet up with another one for comfort and dependent on the temperature you can.

I used this duvet for 7 nights before I could give it an honest review and it is lovely. The only problem I had was that I had the summer one just as the weather was turning cold so I had to put a few layers of blankets on top, which sort of defeated the object.

Had I had a warmer one though I could tell that there were benefits.

The best thing I read is that the dust does not stick to the silk which is why it is great for Asthma sufferers and you do not have to clean the duvets either, which is a bind at the best times. 

They are not cheap but then they do last a very long time so you have to decide that nothing costs too much if you want a sound, easy breathing night of sleep. Which is great for your brain and really sleeping is the best way to help your body stay alert, healthy and fight off any sort of illnesses.

What is great about Silk Bedding Direct is there is a 7 day trial period if you do buy one of these. They have never had a return either and I am not about to send mine back. 

The prices start at £95 for a single, £135 for double and £179 for a king.

They also corporately sponsor the Asthma UK charity.
